Off-Ice Training Guide

How Many Shots Should a Hockey Player Take Per Day?

Short answer: 50 to 150 shots a day, depending on age — done 5 to 6 days a week.

For most youth players, a realistic daily target is about 50 shots a day at U9, rising to 100–150 shots a day by U15 and up. Consistency beats intensity: six focused sessions a week build far more than one giant weekend blast. For younger players, clean mechanics matter more than raw volume.

These are guidance ranges for off-ice driveway/basement practice, not medical or governing-body standards. Build volume gradually and take rest days to avoid overuse strain.

What about the 5,000 and 10,000 shot challenges?

The classic summer challenges are just these daily targets stacked over 8 weeks:

  • 5,000 shots ≈ 625 shots/week ≈ ~90 a day over 7 days. Great for U9–U11 or a first summer.
  • 10,000 shots ≈ 1,250 shots/week ≈ ~180 a day over 7 days. The classic goal for dedicated U13+ players.

You don't need to hit the number every day — you need to not skip. Tracking is what keeps kids honest and motivated, which is exactly why a shared leaderboard works better than a paper sheet taped to the garage wall.

Why consistency beats volume

  • Reps build repeatability. A clean shot at practice speed comes from thousands of correct reps, not a handful of hard ones.
  • Little and often protects growing bodies. High-volume 30-day blasts are where elbow and wrist strains show up. Spread the load.
  • Momentum is motivational. A visible streak and a rising rank keep a 10-year-old going back to the driveway far more than a target number does.
